About Vedanceity

An open research index focused on DOI-first archiving and long-term citation stability for theoretical and interdisciplinary scholarship.

Our mission

Vedanceity curates and indexes DOI-backed research records to make theoretical and interdisciplinary work discoverable, citable, and preserved. We emphasize permanence and provenance: we link to canonical archival records (Zenodo, institutional repositories, and similar services) rather than hosting copies ourselves. This preserves author control and ensures that citations point to authoritative, preservable sources.

Scope & principles

  • We index public records with persistent identifiers (DOIs) from recognized archival services.
  • We prioritize metadata accuracy — title, authors, abstract, date, and canonical archive link are the core fields.
  • Vedanceity is an index and discovery service; we are not a publisher and do not adjudicate peer-review status.

Indexing and permanence

Whenever possible we link to DOI resolver URLs (https://doi.org/...) or the canonical archival record provided by the archive. By relying on DOI-backed archives, Vedanceity supports long-term access and stable citation practices for scholarly outputs.

Community & contributions

Vedanceity welcomes author submissions and community contributions. To request indexing, archive a public record on a supported service and send the DOI link to our contact email. We also accept corrections to metadata (author lists, titles, abstracts, keywords); submissions are reviewed for accuracy before being applied.
